Collections Agent

Monitor overdue AR accounts, AI risk assessment & email drafting, record payments, full audit trail.

What This App Does

This is an accounts receivable (AR) collections management app. It helps collections teams track overdue customer accounts, prioritise follow-ups using AI-generated risk scores, draft personalised collection emails, and record payments — all from a single interface.

There are three core AI-powered actions, each backed by its own automation:

  1. Risk Assessment — AI analyses a customer's invoices, payment history, and overdue patterns to assign a risk score (0–100), risk level (low/medium/high), and a recommended next action. Sets follow-up dates automatically based on urgency.
  2. Email Drafting — AI picks the right reminder template (friendly → firm → urgent → final) based on how overdue the invoices are, then personalises the email with specific invoice details and amounts.
  3. Payment Recording — When a payment comes in, the automation applies it oldest-first across outstanding invoices, updates balances, and automatically resolves the customer's account when fully paid.

The app also includes:

  • Dashboard — Total outstanding, active customer count, follow-ups due today, escalated accounts, AR aging summary (current, 1–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+ days), and priority customers
  • Worklist — Customers sorted by risk score with one-click buttons for assessment and email drafting
  • Customer detail — Three-tab view: Invoices (with balances and days overdue), Activities (email/call/note log), and Payments (history with record-payment form)
  • Audit trail — All changes to customers and invoices are logged automatically via hooks
  • Settings — Reminder email templates by stage, and escalation rules (days overdue / amount thresholds)

Customer Statuses

Customers move through statuses as the collections process progresses:

active contacted promised escalated resolved
  • active — Has outstanding invoices, no recent contact
  • contacted — A collection email has been sent or a call logged
  • promised — Customer has committed to a payment date
  • escalated — Account meets escalation rules (high overdue amount or too many days)
  • resolved — All invoices paid in full (set automatically by the payment automation)

The Three Core Workflows

1. AI Risk Assessment

From the worklist, the user clicks "Assess Risk" on a customer. The ca_assess_risk automation runs, examines all of the customer's invoices and payment history, and returns:

  • Risk score (0–100) — higher means higher risk of non-payment
  • Risk level — low / medium / high
  • Recommended next action — e.g. "Send friendly reminder", "Escalate to manager"
  • Reasoning — Explanation of why this score was assigned

The automation updates the customer's risk_score, risk_level, and next_follow_up date, and logs the assessment as an activity.

2. AI Email Drafting

From the worklist, the user clicks "Draft Email". The ca_draft_email automation:

  1. Looks up the customer's overdue invoices to determine the collection stage
  2. Picks the matching reminder template from ca_reminder_templates:
    • friendly — 1–15 days overdue
    • firm — 16–30 days
    • urgent — 31–60 days
    • final — 60+ days
  3. Uses AI to personalise the template with specific invoice numbers, amounts, and due dates
  4. Saves the draft as an activity on the customer and updates their status to contacted

3. Payment Recording

From the customer detail page (Payments tab), the user enters a payment amount. The ca_record_payment automation:

  1. Creates a ca_payments record
  2. Applies the payment oldest-first — allocates to invoices sorted by due date
  3. Reduces each invoice's balance_remaining accordingly
  4. Updates the customer's total_outstanding
  5. If all invoices are fully paid, automatically sets the customer status to resolved

Automations & Hooks

ca_assess_risk automation

Takes a customer_id. Fetches the customer's invoices and payment history, sends the data to the LLM for analysis, and returns a risk score, level, recommended action, and reasoning. Updates the customer record and logs the assessment as an activity.

External ID: collections-agent:ca_assess_risk

ca_draft_email automation

Takes a customer_id. Determines the collection stage from the customer's most overdue invoice, fetches the matching reminder template, and uses the LLM to personalise it with specific invoice details. Saves the draft as an activity and updates the customer's status to contacted.

External ID: collections-agent:ca_draft_email

ca_record_payment automation

Takes a customer_id and amount. Creates a payment record, applies the amount oldest-first across outstanding invoices, updates each invoice's balance_remaining and paid_amount, recalculates the customer's total_outstanding, and auto-resolves the customer if all balances are zero.

External ID: collections-agent:ca_record_payment

Audit hooks hooks

Three hooks fire on ca_customers after_update and ca_invoices after_create / after_update. They log every change to ca_audit_log with the full before/after state, creating a complete audit trail.

No auto-trigger hooks: Unlike invoice-processing, none of the automations run automatically. They're all triggered explicitly by the user clicking buttons in the UI. This is intentional — collections is a human-in-the-loop process where the user decides when to assess, email, or record payments.

Key Design Decisions

  • Worklist-driven UX: The primary page is a risk-sorted worklist, not a traditional list view. Collections teams work top-down through their highest-risk accounts.
  • Oldest-first payment allocation: When a payment comes in, it's automatically allocated to the oldest unpaid invoices first. This matches standard accounting practice and avoids manual allocation.
  • Template-based email generation: The AI doesn't write emails from scratch — it personalises pre-defined templates. This ensures compliance and consistent tone while still making each email feel personal.
  • Denormalized customer_name on invoices/activities/payments: The customer name is stored directly on related records (not just the ID). This avoids JOINs in list queries and makes the data easier to work with in SQL dashboards.
  • All collections prefixed with ca_: Avoids name collisions with other templates deployed to the same Lumera account.
  • Escalation rules are data, not code: Escalation thresholds are stored in ca_escalation_rules and managed in the settings UI. This lets users adjust escalation policies without code changes.
